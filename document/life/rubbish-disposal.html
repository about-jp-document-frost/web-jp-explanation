1. Strict Separation Rules

Garbage disposal in Japan is highly regulated.

Residents must separate waste into categories such as:

  • Burnable waste
  • Non-burnable waste
  • Plastics
  • Bottles and cans
  • Paper and cardboard

2. Collection Days

Each category has a specific collection day.

Putting out rubbish on the wrong day is not allowed.

When you can take out rubbish depends on the local municipality's schedule.

Even in Tokyo, different wards have different collection days and rules.

Taking out rubbish at night can be prohibited and fined in some areas.

Also, in general, if an apartment has a designated garbage collection area inside, residents can use it at night.

3. Time Restrictions

In many apartments, residents are not allowed to take out garbage at night.

This is because:

  • Animals may tear open the bags
  • Smells may disturb neighbors
  • Noise at night is considered inconsiderate

4. Designated Areas

Garbage must be placed in the designated area or container.

Leaving trash outside the designated spot is strictly prohibited.

5. Community Responsibility

These rules are part of maintaining a clean and orderly living environment.

Residents are expected to follow them carefully.
